Tutorial_week4

This is tutorial slides for week4 ​ The first thing that we discussed in this week is two important theorems, Nyquist’s and Shannon’s theorem, in physical layer. The main difference between them is Nyquist’s theorem assums the channel that we used to transform data is noise-less while the Shannon’s theorem is only used for a noisy channel. They both have two components in their formula, bandwidth and levels of signal.

Tutorial_week3

This is tutorial slides for week3 ​ In this week, we have compared the properties between OSI model and TCP/IP model. They both have a similar stacking protocol achitecture, which holds advantages on flexibility and information hiding (content in week2). In terms of differences, OSI model has a good distinguish between Service, Protocol and Interface. In the meanwhile, TCP/IP doesn’t. ​ Service defines what the process does, but not how entities above it access it or how the layer works.
